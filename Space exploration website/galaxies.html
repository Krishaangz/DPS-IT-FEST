Galaxies

What are Galaxies?

Galaxies are massive systems composed of stars, star clusters, planetary systems, gas, dust, and dark matter. They can range in size from dwarfs with just a few billion stars to the largest galaxies known, which contain one hundred trillion stars or more. They are the building blocks of the universe, interacting through gravitational forces, and can be classified into various types such as spiral, elliptical, and irregular.

Recent Discoveries in Galaxies

Recent observations from telescopes such as the James Webb Space Telescope have unveiled new classes of galaxies that challenge our understanding of cosmic evolution. For instance, astronomers have discovered ultra-diffuse galaxies that, despite their size, contain very few stars, suggesting unique formation processes that could shed light on the nature of dark matter and the evolution of galaxies. Furthermore, ongoing research aims to explore the role of supermassive black holes at the centers of galaxies and their influence on star formation.
