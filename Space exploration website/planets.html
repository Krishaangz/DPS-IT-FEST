Planets

Planets are diverse celestial bodies orbiting stars, each unique in composition, structure, and environmental conditions. From rocky planets to gas giants, the variety in planetary types across the universe enriches our understanding of cosmic diversity.

Categories of Planets

Rocky Planets

These planets have solid, rocky surfaces and are typically smaller. Examples include Earth and Mars.

Rogue Planets

Rogue planets wander through space without a star, likely ejected from their original systems.

Gas Giants

Massive planets with thick atmospheres primarily composed of hydrogen and helium, like Jupiter.

Dwarf Planets

Small celestial bodies in orbit around the Sun, such as Pluto, that do not dominate their orbits.

Ice Giants

Planets with thick atmospheres rich in water, ammonia, and methane, like Neptune and Uranus.
