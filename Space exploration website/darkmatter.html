Dark Matter Discoveries

Dark matter remains one of the most intriguing mysteries in astrophysics. Recent discoveries have shed light on its properties and interactions, significantly enhancing our understanding of the universe.

Types of Dark Matter Discoveries

Direct Detection

Recent experiments have made significant strides in detecting dark matter particles directly, providing essential insights into their properties.

Gravitational Lensing

Observations of gravitational lensing effects reveal the presence of dark matter in galaxy clusters, helping to map its distribution in the universe.

Cosmic Microwave Background

New analyses of the cosmic microwave background radiation provide crucial data about dark matter's role in the early universe.

Galaxy Rotation Curves

Studies of galaxy rotation curves continue to support the existence of dark matter, revealing discrepancies between visible and expected mass.

Bullet Cluster

The Bullet Cluster provides compelling evidence for dark matter through the separation of visible mass from gravitational effects.

Simulations and Models

Advanced simulations and models of galaxy formation continue to refine our understanding of dark matter's role in cosmic evolution.
