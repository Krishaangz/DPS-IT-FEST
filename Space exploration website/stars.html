Stars

Stars are luminous celestial bodies composed primarily of hydrogen and helium, producing energy through nuclear fusion. They vary in size, brightness, and lifespan, enriching our galaxy with their presence and radiance.

Categories of Stars

Main Sequence Stars

Stars that fuse hydrogen into helium in their cores, like our Sun.

Red Giants

Expanding stars that have exhausted hydrogen in their cores, appearing red and massive.

White Dwarfs

Small, dense remnants of stars that have shed their outer layers.

Neutron Stars

Extremely dense stars formed from the remnants of massive stars after a supernova.

Supernova Stars

Massive explosions that mark the death of massive stars, scattering elements across the universe.

Pulsars

Highly magnetized, rotating neutron stars that emit beams of electromagnetic radiation.
