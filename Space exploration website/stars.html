Stars

Stars are massive luminous celestial bodies made of plasma, primarily hydrogen and helium, generating energy through nuclear fusion. They play a vital role in the universe, forming the building blocks of galaxies and providing energy for planetary systems.

Categories of Stars

Main Sequence Stars

The most common type of stars, including the Sun, generating energy by fusing hydrogen into helium.

Red Giants

Large, luminous stars nearing the end of their life, having expanded after exhausting hydrogen in their core.

White Dwarfs

Dense remnants of stars that have exhausted nuclear fuel, slowly cooling over billions of years.

Neutron Stars

Incredibly dense stars, formed from supernova remnants, composed primarily of neutrons.

Supergiants

Extremely massive and luminous stars, representing an advanced stage in stellar evolution.

Binary Stars

Systems of two stars orbiting each other, often influencing each other's evolution and properties.
