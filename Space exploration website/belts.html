Asteroid Belts

Asteroid belts are regions in space where a large number of asteroids or small celestial bodies are concentrated. These belts can be found in various solar systems, including our own, where they serve as the boundary between planets and the outer regions of the solar system.

Categories of Asteroid Belts

Asteroid Belt of Our Solar System

The region between Mars and Jupiter where most of the solar system's asteroids are located.

Kuiper Belt

A region beyond Neptune filled with icy bodies, dwarf planets, and comets.

Oort Cloud

Beyond the Kuiper Belt, this spherical cloud is made up of icy bodies that could be considered a distant asteroid belt.

Main Belt of the Jupiter Trojans

A group of asteroids sharing Jupiter's orbit, forming two distinct groups known as the L4 and L5 Lagrange points.

Centaurs

Celestial bodies that share characteristics of both asteroids and comets, typically found between Jupiter and Neptune.

Trojan Asteroids of Neptune

Asteroids that share Neptune's orbit, trapped in stable positions 60 degrees ahead of and behind the planet.
